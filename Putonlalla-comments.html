Comments on The Simple Essence of Automatic Differentiation

Here are some comments on and off the paper (revision 2018-03-07). I hope at least some value can be extracted from them.

  1. My introductory calculus class (back then called real analysis) was a bit different from yours as the definition of the derivative of a function was initially mixed up with the Weierstrass definition of a limit. It was a mess.

  2. Using A=BA=B A = B for definitional (judgmental) equality and A≡BA≡B A \equiv B for ordinary (propositional) equality bothers me a bit, because their meanings are swapped in type theoretical texts such as Homotopy Type Theory: Univalent Foundations of Mathematics.

  3. I like the less simplified definition f'x=limε→0f(x+ε)−fxid(x+ε)−idxf′x=limε→0f(x+ε)−fxid(x+ε)−idx \begin{align}f' x & = \lim_{\varepsilon\to 0}\frac{f (x + \varepsilon) - f x}{\operatorname{id}(x + \varepsilon) - \operatorname{id}x}\end{align} for its visual appeal.

  4. The “symmetry condition” of the Hessian matrix is known as “Schwarz’s theorem”.

  5. Even as a Haskell programmer, I do not particularly enjoy overloading the names of types and terms.

  6. Perhaps it would be good to explain what “compositional” really means before noting that the naive chain rule is, in fact, not compositional.

  7. The sentence featuring “is assembled entirely components of” is grammatically questionable.

  8. The “cross rule” looks like “chain homotopy equivalence” found in literature. Perhaps texts that talk about bifunctors also have a name for it.

  9. I did not know “abutment” is a word. It seems to be later replaced by “juxtaposition” anyway.

  10. Regarding your remark in the footnotes, I cannot comment on the readability of a `k` b, because I have been tainted by Haskell, but perhaps one could mention it can be read as a→kba→kb a \to_k b or ak→ba→kb a \to\limits^k b.

  11. I have noticed that using the same membership notation for both the objects and the morphisms of a category with internal homs can be confusing. This may not be an issue in this paper, but one should still be wary of it.

  12. Regarding your remark in the footnotes, do the simple proofs for identity and associativity arise from the fact that the functor instance is uniquely determined by parametricity? Am I missing something as well?

  13. The sentence featuring “guides correct-by-construction of” is grammatically questionable.

  14. Perhaps it would be useful to number or explicitly name some of the equations (or listings) instead of referring to them by informal names such as “the monoidal functor property”. I must admit that I mixed “left-hand sides” with “right-hand sides” more than once.

  15. I think the class definition for Cocartesian should not feature Additive c.

  16. The sentence featuring “shared between the a function” is grammatically questionable.

  17. Section 7 is lost on me.

  18. The sections from 8 to 11 feel more like a collection of remarks than a story. I think they do not fit the otherwise cohesive narrative of the paper.

  19. The sentence featuring “f'::U⊸V.f′::U⊸V. f' :: U \multimap V_.” has a strange (and very tiny) subscript.

  20. Why is the following true?

    Considering the nature of the sparse vectors used as arguments, each pass likely computes inefficiently.

  21. It is funny that the text contains the phrase “pp p values”, because now statisticians might accidentally find a paper on functional programming.

  22. The sentence featuring “the main purposes of this paper” is grammatically questionable.

  23. The notation DContR(→+)DCont(→+)ℝ D_{{Cont}_{(\to^+)}^{\mathbb R}} is quite something.

  24. The reverse and forward modes could do with more exposition. Perhaps a side-by-side comparison of the resulting diagrams would be in order.

  25. I wonder in what situations mixed mode would be useful. Never? Future work.

  26. In my personal experience, algorithms implemented as GHC plugins are very fragile to changes in GHC itself and both the code and error messages are incomprehensible to those unfamiliar with GHC internals. For a research project GHC seems to be a great vehicle, but I am left pondering whether another language or implementation method would suit these concepts better. Tactics? Elaborator reflection? Future work.

  27. I admit skipping most of the appendices.

  28. Good paper.
